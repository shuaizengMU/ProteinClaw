## Design Context

### Users
Biotech industry professionals (drug discovery, genomics, research teams) working in corporate R&D environments. They use ProteinClaw during focused research sessions to answer complex protein biology questions through natural language queries. They need efficiency, accuracy, and trust in the results.

### Brand Personality
Scientific, rigorous, precise. The interface should reflect the seriousness of the research being conducted—no frivolous elements, no playfulness. Every visual choice should serve the data and workflow, not decoration.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Minimal & stark**: Maximum clarity, zero decorative elements. The interface should get out of the way and let the research results speak. Stark means confident without being cold—clean geometry, clear hierarchy, but not sterile. Think laboratory environment: necessary equipment only, precise layout, zero visual noise.

**Anti-references**: No gradient accents, no glowing effects, no glossy cards, no unnecessary rounded corners. No "modern SaaS" defaults. No animation for decoration. No AI slop tells.

### Visual Principles
1. **Data first**: Every visual element must serve the research or workflow
2. **Precision over polish**: Stark doesn't mean rough—attention to alignment, spacing, and contrast
3. **Trust through clarity**: Users need to trust what they're seeing; ambiguous UI undermines scientific confidence
4. **Dark + light**: Both modes equally rigorous; dark for extended sessions, light for daytime lab work
5. **No decoration**: If it's not functional, it's clutter

### Current Strengths
- Design tokens in CSS (proper theming system)
- Both light/dark modes with consistent palette
- Warm neutral palette (off-white/dark) instead of sterile grays
- Good semantic spacing structure
- Clean component layout

### Known Issues (for audit to assess)
- System fonts (no distinctive typography)
- Warm accent color may feel less "scientific" than cooler alternatives
- Need to verify contrast ratios for accessibility
- Check for any unnecessary decorative elements
- Ensure responsive design works on various screen sizes
- Validate animation use (should be functional only)
